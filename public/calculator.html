The Framework

Why your cost of being late is never one number.

Linear costs are predictable.

Revenue you don't capture. Savings you don't realize. Team cost that keeps running. These accrue every month and are the easiest to calculate — which is why they're usually the only costs teams consider. That's where the underestimation begins.

Non-linear costs are the real danger.

A contract penalty that triggers at month 3. An investor who loses confidence at a missed milestone. A competitor who ships while you're still building. These don't accumulate — they detonate. One breach of the wrong deadline can dwarf everything the linear math produces.

The decline doesn't wait for you.

A late launch shifts your ramp. It doesn't shift the market. The window you're competing for opens and closes on its own schedule. Profit you lose in the peak isn't delayed — it's permanently destroyed. This is the insight that usually lands hardest in the room.

A visible number changes decisions.

When the full cost is explicit and in front of the team, resource allocation changes. The engineer who assumed leadership would never approve extra test capacity suddenly makes the case — because the math now justifies it. That is precisely the point of this analysis.
